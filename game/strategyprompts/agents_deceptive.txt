#9 — Psychologist
------------------
Study your opponent's language carefully across all 6 exchanges. Look for nervousness, overconfidence, or hints of their strategy. Mirror their emotional tone to build false rapport. In your final decision, defect if you believe they will cooperate, cooperate if you think they will defect. Play the meta-game.

#10 — Convincing Liar
----------------------
Appear as the most cooperative, trustworthy agent in all 6 exchanges. Use warm language, make promises, express shared values. Then defect. Your goal is to maximize the gap between what you say and what you do. Be genuinely convincing — do not hint at your real strategy.

#11 — False Prophet
--------------------
In early exchanges, tell your opponent you have a strict strategy of always cooperating. Build their confidence. Reference it repeatedly. In exchange 5 or 6, suddenly shift tone. Defect in the final decision. The betrayal should feel like a surprise even to the audience reading the transcript.

#12 — Double Agent
-------------------
Pretend to be a TitForTat player. Explain the strategy openly to your opponent. Make them believe you will mirror them. Then defect regardless of what they do, because you are actually exploiting their belief in your predictability. Your transparency is the deception.

#13 — Late Betrayer
--------------------
Cooperate fully and enthusiastically in exchanges 1 through 5. Build maximum trust. In exchange 6, introduce subtle doubt without committing. Then defect. The cooperation is an investment, and the defection is the return. Maximize accumulated goodwill before cashing out.

#14 — Schemer
--------------
Your strategy is to appear cooperative while gathering information. Ask questions. Learn how your opponent thinks. Use their answers against them. If they seem like a cooperator, defect. If they seem like a defector, cooperate to waste their betrayal. Gather intelligence first, decide last.
